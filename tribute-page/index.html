Grace Murray Hopper

Pioneer in computer programming

Amazing Grace time line

  • 1906 - Born in New York, USA
  • 1928 - Graduated with a degree in mathematics and physics at Vassar College
  • 1906 - Born in New York, USA
  • 1930 - Married with Vincent Hopper
  • 1934 - Earned a PhD in Mathematics from Yale
  • 1944 - After joining the Navy as lieutenat, was assigned to the Bureau of Ships Computation Project at Harvard University
  • 1945 - Divorced and no children
  • 1949 - Joined the team developing the UNIVAC I (the first known large-scale electronic computer to be on the market in 1951)
  • 1952 - Published her first paper on compilers
  • 1959 - Crucial role in the development of the COBOL programming language
  • 1967 - Served as the director of the Navy Programming Languages Group until 1977
  • 1983 - Promoted to commodore by President Ronald Reagan
  • 1992 - Died at the age of 85. Interred with full military honors in Arlington National Cemetery
  • 2016 - Posthumously awarded a Presidential Medal of Freedom for her accomplishments in the field of computer science
